Symposium Presentation Options

At the symposium this year, there are three options for participating: the traditional longer format talk, the short format talk, and the infographic contest. We suggest that graduate students select only one talk format, but a speaker may also submit an infographic. Prizes will be awarded within each of the three categories.

Long Format Talk


This traditional-style prospective or retrospective presentation will be 8 minutes long with 3 minutes for questions and answers. Presenters will have access to a projector and department computer.

Category Summary:

  • Time Limit: 12 minutes
  • Audience: University faculty and grad students
  • Media: Projector with connected laptop

Expanding urban forestry by expanding our vision: Understanding the many dialogues about trees and people in Detroit, Michigan

Farmers' Attitudes and Decision-Making in Response to Climate Change in Smallholder Agroforestry Systems in Southeastern Senegal

Evaluation of the Current National Woodland Owner Survey Estimator for Private Forest Landowners: A Case Study of Montana

Machine learning techniques for quantifying downed woody debris in Tanana Valley, Alaska

Long-term effects of prescribed burning on soil properties in red pine ecosystems

Wildfire severity impacts on post-fire soil carbon storage and mineralization

Modeling rates of biomass and nutrient accumulation in high-density jack pine plantations of the Lake States Region

Variable Effects of Climate on Individual Tree Growth in Relation to Ecosystem State

Estimating Tree Leaf Mass Using Shade Tolerance Ratings

How a warming experiment is used to determine invasive plant success: considering biotic interactions for the future

Understanding the roles of fungi in tree seedling light-gradient partitioning

White Pine Decline in Michigan

Short Format Talk


The short format talks give you the opportunity to present your research in a way you find most appropriate. In addition to the shorter time for presenting, the focus of these short talks will be on communicating the the public, non-scientists.

Category Summary:

  • Time Limit: 7 minutes
  • Audience: General public
  • Media: Projector with connected laptop OR whiteboard OR nothing

Infographic Contest


"If policymakers and those trying to affect policy can’t effectively communicate their ideas—and visualization is a key tool in those efforts—it will be hard to convert those ideas into improved policy."

Jon Schwabish, policyviz.com, interviewed in June, 03, 2014


Infographic Topic Ideas

  • Explain the significance of your research on different scales.
  • Explain a more complicated aspect of your research like a method.
  • Describe your research process or interests.
  • Make a timeline of relevant research or discoveries related to your research.
  • Describe a species that is integral to your research.
  • Visually represent data or results relevant to your research.
