2015 Introduction

Holding today’s Symposium in Chittenden Hall carries special significance. Chittenden was the home of the Forestry Department from 1913 until 1966. ‘Forestry’ is engraved in leaf motif in the stone lintel above Chittenden Hall’s main entrance. Now occupied by the Graduate School, Chittenden Hall’s stone engraving symbolizes the tight connection between graduate education and the Department of Forestry. Graduate education is an important part of our mission and graduate students play a critical role in carrying out the Department’s research as well as outreach and teaching.

My hope is that today’s symposium will continue to facilitate learning about each other’s research and lead to new interdisciplinary collaborations, as we continue to tackle some of the world’s most pressing natural resource questions.

Dr. Richard Kobe, as an introduction to the 2015 symposium
